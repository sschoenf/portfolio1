Pitch

Quiksilver Reach

We are proposing an athletic performance digital application called Quiksilver Reach made for aspiring surfers.

Reach is designed to visualize performance insights by identifying patterns in your surf sessions to make improving your technique easier. Through this implementation, our goal is to bring Quiksilver back into the forefront of the surfing community.

Business Problem

Quiksilver

Quiksilver is a well known brand, however they declared bankruptcy in 2015. We wanted to investigate what lead to this outcome and how we could intervene to help.

“Quiksilver diverged from the needs and wants of its core customer, the athlete” -(Carroll, 2015).

Through our research into Quiksilver we identified that it was diversifying its products to a wider audience, however this emphasis on cross selling diluted their core brand value of being authentic to board sports and culture.

“Quiksilver suffered a 13% decline in sales in 2015 with its net loss widened to 309.4 million” -(Bloomberg, 2015).

Due to their diluted brand values, Quiksilver was being misperceived as a fashion brand rather than an authentic board-sport brand, thus ultimately leading them file for bankruptcy.

Frame

Intervention

With the understanding that Quiksilver was diverging from their core values and losing their athlete consumers, we concluded that in order to re-captivate their customer base, they need to clarify their original message by refocusing on surfers, who are their core audience.

To help us re-frame on how to approach this problem we asked ourselves:

How can Quiksilver use technology to support athletes in achieving their goals by measuring their progress?

+

How can we make performance data easier to understand and use to better support aspiring surfers?

Solution

Athletic Performance Application

Our solution is to create an athletic performance digital application tailored for aspiring surfers by acquiring Trace technology.

*Trace is a device and app that provides the technology to track data specific to surfers. The device attaches to the surfboard and collects data from your surf, while the app presents it.

Since Trace is a small startup, Quiksilver’s acquisition with Trace positions themselves more competitively against rivals who don’t offer performance data and video footage, which are features unique to Trace. Quiksilver can refocus on their surfing heritage by leveraging Trace’s technology for the next generation of surfers

Persona

Lukas, Aspiring surfer

To help us identify key touchpoints for our target user and to understand what they value we created the persona of Lukas. We created a video with the purpose of explaining Lukas’s motivations and values, as an aspiring surfer, to a non-surfer audience. By doing this it helped us better illustrate the value of our application.

Lukas is a 17 year old surfer aspiring to become a professional athlete.

Recap

Quiksilver Reach

Quiksilver Reach supports the next generation of aspiring pro surfers like Lukas, who needs professional level training to become a sponsored athlete. By connecting Quiksilver with a younger generation of their core surfing audience, Quiksilver can position themselves back into the forefront of the surfing community.

UI Process

Quiksilver Reach

Trace’s UI is overloaded with raw data and lacks hierarchy which makes it more difficult for surfers to understand. In our research we found that once an athlete has their data, how it’s visualized is critical to how much they can use and benefit from it.

We reduced the amount of information shown at once and improved the visual hierarchy so that the surfer see what is most important to them such as their top distance, speed, or air height. They are also given a quick,clean, visual overview of their waves with the option to get more details further below, thus reducing the cognitive overload with too much information being presented at once

Reflection

Personal Insights

This project allowed us to further explore experience design with the added challenge of incorporating a physical product with a digital product. We had to consider multiple touchpoints and constantly had to adjust our methods in order to create a seamless experience. My main take-away from this project was realizing the importance of creating persona’s to better understand who you are designing for. I have never surfed in my life before, but through my research I was able to distinguish what information would actually be relevant to a surfer. Going forward I will continue to create persona’s so that my design decisions are valuable to the end user.
